register: product

## Product purpose
A fully local desktop tool that transcribes recorded interviews with children EXACTLY as
spoken (invented words, mispronunciations, ungrammatical forms, false starts). It renders the
literal acoustic truth, paired with a smoother "readable" reference. Audio of minors never
leaves the machine. The literal text is the primary artifact; the readable layer is a muted
reference aid only.

## Users
A non-technical educational/clinical professional (speech therapist, pedagogist, educator)
reviewing recordings at a desk during the workday. They read and correct transcriptions
carefully, comparing text against what the child actually said. No terminal exposure; one
window, drag and drop. Italian UI.

## Tone
Calm, trustworthy, precise. The tool disappears into the task. Document-review feel, like
marking up a manuscript, not a flashy media player. Serious and clinical without feeling cold
or "medical".

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS blue-on-white dashboard.
- Dark "audio app" aesthetic: waveforms, neon accents, black panels.
- Medical-teal clinical cliche.
- Card grids and hero-metric templates.

## Strategic principles
- The literal layer must visually dominate; the readable layer must be unmistakably secondary.
- Zero network at runtime: NO web fonts, no CDN assets. System and web-safe stacks only.
- Familiarity and consistency over surprise. Standard affordances, predictable layout.
- Every state present: hover, focus, active, disabled, loading, saved, error, empty.
